# Privacy Policy — Productivity Blocker

**Last updated: June 13, 2026**

Productivity Blocker is designed to protect your privacy. It does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data.

## What the extension stores

The extension saves the following **locally**, using Chrome's `storage.sync` API:

- Your configured work hours (start and end time).
- Which sites you have enabled or disabled for blocking.
- A **salted, hashed** version of your bypass password (the password itself is never stored).
- The expiry timestamp of an active bypass, if any.

## Where that data goes

- All of the above stays within your own browser. If you are signed into Chrome with sync enabled, `storage.sync` may replicate these settings across your own devices through your Google account — this is handled entirely by Chrome and is never accessible to the developer.
- The extension contains **no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and no remote servers**. Nothing you do is sent to the developer or any third party.

## Permissions

The extension uses `declarativeNetRequest`, `storage`, `alarms`, and host access limited to the specific sites it blocks or modifies. These are used solely to provide the blocking functionality described in the README. They are not used to collect data.
