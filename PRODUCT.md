# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One person at a desk during their own declared work hours (default 9–5), usually mid-task when they hit a wall the extension put up. They are not "using" this product so much as being gently policed by it — the popup and options are visited rarely; the blocked page is seen often, at moments of weak will. Secondary surface: quick status checks from the toolbar popup.

## Product Purpose

Productivity Blocker enforces focus during user-configured work hours: full blocks on distracting domains, feed/recommendation blanking on YouTube/LinkedIn/Facebook/Twitter, TikTok videos blocked around the clock (except TikTok Studio), and a password-gated 15-minute bypass. Success = the user stays on task and the tool never feels hostile enough to uninstall.

## Brand Personality

Calm, warm, disciplined. A quiet coach, not a scolding parent. The blocked page should read as "not now" rather than "DENIED". Emotional goal: the user exhales and goes back to work, without shame or friction.

## Anti-references

- Alarm-red "ACCESS DENIED" blocker pages (Cold Turkey / corporate-firewall energy).
- Dark techno-dashboard slate-and-indigo (the previous design; also the default AI extension look).
- Gamified streak/guilt mechanics and mascots.

## Design Principles

1. **Not now, not never** — blocking copy is temporal ("can wait", "until 5:00 PM"), never punitive.
2. **The wall is quiet** — the blocked page is the most-seen surface; it earns whitespace and one serif sentence, not warnings and icons.
3. **Familiar controls, no ceremony** — settings and popup use standard, dense, obvious affordances; the tool disappears into the task.
4. **One warm voice** — a single amber brand color carries every action and state emphasis; everything else stays neutral.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. All interactive states keyboard-reachable with visible focus. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored (the error shake becomes a static message). No color-only state signaling — status pills carry text.
